Aptitude Course

Learn logical reasoning, problem-solving, and quantitative skills to excel in your entrance exams.

Important Logical Reasoning Concepts:

In this section, we will focus on critical topics in logical reasoning and problem-solving. Below is an overview of key areas to master:

1. Number Series

Identify patterns in sequences of numbers to predict the next number in the series.

  • Example: 2, 4, 6, 8, ? (Answer: 10, as the pattern is +2).
  • Practice different types of series, including arithmetic, geometric, and mixed sequences.

2. Coding and Decoding

Understand the logic behind encoding and decoding words or numbers.

  • Example: If CAT is coded as DBU, what is DOG? (Answer: EPH, as each letter shifts by +1).

3. Blood Relations

Analyze relationships between family members based on given information.

  • Example: "John is the brother of Mary. Mary is the daughter of Sam." What is John’s relation to Sam? (Answer: Son).

4. Syllogisms

Deduce conclusions from a set of premises using logical reasoning.

  • Example: All cats are animals. Some animals are dogs. Conclusion: Some dogs are cats? (Answer: False).

5. Time and Distance

Learn to calculate time, speed, and distance in various scenarios.

  • Formula: Distance = Speed × Time.
  • Example: A car travels at 60 km/h for 2 hours. How far does it travel? (Answer: 120 km).

6. Permutations and Combinations

Understand arrangements and selections of items.

  • Permutation: Arrangement of items where order matters.
  • Combination: Selection of items where order doesn’t matter.
  • Example: How many ways can you arrange the letters A, B, C? (Answer: 3! = 6).

7. Probability

Learn to calculate the likelihood of events occurring.

  • Formula: Probability = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes.
  • Example: What is the probability of rolling a 4 on a fair die? (Answer: 1/6).

8. Ratio and Proportion

Master solving problems involving ratios and proportions.

  • Example: If 3 pens cost $6, how much do 5 pens cost? (Answer: $10).

9. Profit and Loss

Understand how to calculate profit, loss, and their percentages.

  • Profit: Selling Price - Cost Price.
  • Loss: Cost Price - Selling Price.
  • Example: If a product is bought for $50 and sold for $70, what is the profit? (Answer: $20).

10. Work and Time

Learn to calculate how long it takes to complete tasks based on given work rates.

  • Example: If A can complete a task in 5 hours and B can complete it in 10 hours, how long will it take for them to complete it together? (Answer: 3.33 hours).

By mastering these concepts, you will enhance your problem-solving skills and perform better in aptitude exams. Practice regularly and analyze your mistakes to improve further. Good luck on your journey!
