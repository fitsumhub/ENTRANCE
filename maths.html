Mathematics Course

Learn algebra, geometry, and calculus to excel in your entrance exams.

Key Mathematics Topics:

This course covers the essential mathematics concepts you need for your exams. Below is an overview of the main topics:

1. Algebra

Algebra involves solving equations and understanding relationships between variables.

  • Linear Equations: Equations of the form ax + b = c. Example: Solve for x in 2x + 3 = 7.
  • Quadratic Equations: Equations of the form ax² + bx + c = 0. Example: Solve x² - 5x + 6 = 0.
  • Inequalities: Solve and graph inequalities like x + 3 > 5.
  • Functions: Understand concepts like domain, range, and graphing functions.

2. Geometry

Geometry focuses on shapes, sizes, and the properties of space.

  • Triangles: Study properties of right, isosceles, and equilateral triangles.
  • Circles: Learn about radius, diameter, circumference, and area.
  • Angles: Measure angles and understand complementary and supplementary angles.
  • Pythagoras' Theorem: For right triangles, a² + b² = c².

3. Trigonometry

Trigonometry deals with relationships between the angles and sides of triangles.

  • Sine, Cosine, Tangent: Learn the trigonometric ratios and their applications.
  • Unit Circle: Understand how angles correspond to coordinates on a circle.
  • Trigonometric Identities: Simplify expressions using identities like sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.

4. Calculus

Calculus is the study of change, focusing on derivatives and integrals.

  • Limits: Understand how functions behave as they approach a specific value.
  • Derivatives: Learn to calculate the rate of change of a function. Example: f'(x) for f(x) = x² is 2x.
  • Integrals: Learn to find areas under curves using definite and indefinite integrals.

5. Probability and Statistics

Understand data analysis and probability concepts.

  • Probability: Calculate the likelihood of events. Example: Rolling a 6 on a die.
  • Mean, Median, Mode: Learn to summarize data sets.
  • Standard Deviation: Measure the spread of data around the mean.

6. Matrices

Matrices involve arrays of numbers used to solve systems of equations and other applications.

  • Matrix Addition: Add matrices of the same dimensions.
  • Matrix Multiplication: Multiply matrices following specific rules.
  • Determinants and Inverses: Learn to calculate determinants and use matrix inverses for solving equations.

7. Sequences and Series

Study patterns and sums of sequences.

  • Arithmetic Sequences: Example: 2, 4, 6, 8 (common difference = 2).
  • Geometric Sequences: Example: 3, 6, 12, 24 (common ratio = 2).
  • Series: Find sums of sequences using formulas.

Review these topics, practice problems, and refer to this guide as needed. Best of luck on your entrance exam preparation!
